Family Health Initiative Ogun

FAHIO is a registered non-governmental Organization that is into advocacy on family health, with special interest in the health of women and children. FAHIO has within the years influenced government policies that has helped the State increase its health indices as well as reduction in the maternal mortality rate in Ogun state.

Our Story

FAHIO (Family Health Initiative Ogun) was founded with a simple yet powerful belief: every family deserves access to quality healthcare, regardless of their economic status or geographical location.

What began as a small community health project has grown into a comprehensive family health initiative serving thousands of families. We recognized that healthcare is not just about treating illness, but about building healthy communities where families can thrive together.

Through mobile health clinics, health education programs, maternal and child health services, and community outreach, we've made a tangible difference in countless lives. Our team of dedicated healthcare professionals, volunteers, and community health workers work tirelessly to ensure that no family is left behind.

Today, FAHIO stands as a beacon of hope for underserved communities, but our work is far from over. Every day, we recommit ourselves to our mission of making quality family healthcare accessible to all.

AIMS AND OBJECTIVES

Education

To provide free qualitative healthcare services to the vulnerable and poor rural dwellers

Healthcare

To identify health needs and problem of each family with the mindset of preferring solution to them

Community Participation

To plan and provide healthcare services with the active participation of families

Independence

To help families develop abilities to deal with basic health needs and health problem independently

Upliftment and Growth

To contribute to families' upliftment and growth

Proper Counselling

To educate, counsel, and guide family members to cultivate good personal health habit

Empowerment

To empower Nigerians, particularly the poor and vulnerable to live healthier life

Evaluation

To provide periodic and continuous appraisal and evaluation of family health situation
