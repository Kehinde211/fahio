5 ESSENTIAL HEALTH CHECKS EVERY MOTHER NEEDS

Mothers are a special one in our lives. They wake up before the rest of the house does. They clean the house and make food for everyone. Still, they find a way to show up for work in the morning, despite the stress they've undergone. They are the ones who know everyone's peculiar behaviors, everyone's favourite meal, and exactly how each person in the family wants their food to be dished. They give and continue to give quietly, consistently, without waiting to be asked.

And yet, this begs the question: when was the last time they were asked how they are doing?

Not the casual "how are you" that everyone just says light off the lips. The kind that comes from a thoughtful place. One that involves self-care, a proper check-up, and honest answers. For many mothers, especially in Nigeria, self-care is a dream. It feels selfish, or unnecessary, or simply something to get to later. But later has a way of never coming into reality.

The numbers tell a revealing story. Ogun State faces significant maternal health challenges, with a maternal mortality ratio of 179 deaths per 100,000 live births due to pregnancy-related issues. Neonatal mortality stands at 56 per 1,000 live births, the highest in Southwest Nigeria per the 2021 UNICEF Multiple Indicator Cluster Survey, compared to Lagos (11), Osun (12), and Ondo (18). These are not just statistics. They are mothers. And they deserve better.

This Mother's Day, the most meaningful thing everyone can do, from a place of care and love for every mother, is show up for their health. Here are five checks that deserve to be prioritised:

1. Blood Pressure Check
It takes less than two minutes and it could save their lives. High blood pressure rarely announces itself. There is no pain, no obvious symptoms, which is exactly what makes it so easy to miss and dismiss. The only way to find out if you have high blood pressure is to have it checked regularly. For any mother juggling stress, stunted sleep or even insomnia, and a routine that never stops, this is one number she simply cannot afford not to know.

2. Breast Cancer Screening
Breast cancer is one of the most common cancers in women, and the earlier it is found, the better it can be avoided or managed. All women should start getting mammograms every one to two years beginning at age 40, though those with a family history may need to start sooner. It is a brief, uncomfortable few minutes that could make all the difference. If you as a mother have been postponing, this is the reminder to stop postponing.

3. Cervical Cancer Screening (Pap Smear + HPV Test)
Cervical cancer is largely preventable, but only when women actually show up for their screenings. A Pap smear detects abnormal cells on the cervix, while HPV testing checks for high-risk strains of the human papillomavirus that can trigger those changes. The guidance is straightforward: start at age 21, with a Pap smear every three years through age 29, then combine it with an HPV test every five years from ages 30 to 65. A few minutes on a table, potentially years added to a life.

4. Cholesterol and Blood Sugar Check
Heart disease remains the leading cause of death in women, yet many mothers have never once had a cholesterol conversation with their doctor. A straightforward blood test can flag cardiovascular risk long before symptoms ever appear. Blood sugar matters too, particularly for mothers who experienced gestational diabetes, as the risk does not simply disappear after delivery. High blood sugar and high cholesterol may not produce any symptoms in the early stages, but a simple blood test can catch both conditions before they become serious.

5. Bone Density Scan
This scan tends to be overlooked until a fracture makes it impossible to ignore. Women begin losing bone density as early as their 30s, and that loss accelerates sharply after menopause when oestrogen levels fall. Osteoporosis is quiet by nature, no aches, no warning, until something breaks. Screening is recommended for women whose fracture risk matches that of an average 65-year-old, factoring in things like family history, smoking, or low body weight. Catching it early means there is still time to reverse the damage before it worsens.

And to Every Mother Reading This:

You remember every single thing, not just yours, but those that concerns the others. You serve your family well, ensure the kids are taken care of, and sit in waiting rooms for the people you love. Now it is your turn.

Book the appointment. Take the tests. Let someone look after you for once, because the people in your life need you here, healthy and whole, for as long as possible.

To Every Father Reading This
You know her better than everyone. You married her. You have watched her push through exhaustion, put herself last, and still smile through all the stress. This is your moment to return the favour and make her feel seen.

Book the appointment for her if she won't or forgets to do it herself. Take her to the hospital. Sit in the waiting room. Ask the doctor the questions she might be too busy or too proud to ask. Be the reason she shows up for herself.

A mother who is healthy is a gift to everyone around her. And you, more than anyone, have the power to make that happen.

This Mother's Day, love her the way she has always loved your family—quietly, consistently, and without waiting to be asked.

Happy International Mother's Day.
